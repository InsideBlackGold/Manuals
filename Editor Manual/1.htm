1. Creating a simple game layout

  
                   
  New Map  
Click on "New" ( on the right hand side of the status bar ). A window  opens up  where the general parameters of the game can be defined 
                   
  You have the following options:  
 
- Level name
( defines at which  level the game  is   to be played ),
 File name-  (Here the layout can be saved on the hard disk),
  
- Map size 
(from the smallest - 32 x 32 - to the largest -256 x 256),
- Type of surface (on which the game is to be played),
- Sea level is the last option in this window. Here you can set how high above sea level the game is to  be played.
                   
  Type of Terrain  
                   
  After setting the general parameters, the type of terrain can be defined by "bending" the basic flat playing area. This can be done manually, using the "Altitudes" tab, or with the built-in map generators located on the "Generator" tab.  
                   
   
     
                   
  To make the map look even more realistic, you can use the "Generate Noise" option. This  generates slight ripples on the surface.  
                   
   
                   
   
                   
  Insert Textures  
                   
  You can texture any game area manually using the various tools that can be found under the "Textures" tab. If you want this done automatically, go to the "Generators" tab and click on "Generate Textures".  
                   
   
                   
  Resources  
                   
  Required raw materials must be distributed over the game area. To do this, first use the tab "Special", then the tab "Resources". Point to a location and left-click. The longer you hold down the mouse, the more oil will be distributed at that location. You’ll see two information pointers on this tab: the left pointer informs you of the total amount of raw materials in the game area and the right pointer tells you the size of the current oil field.  
  The amount of raw materials in a particular field can be reduced by holding down the left hand mouse button and the SHIFT key simultaneously. "Brush Size" will expand the area in which the raw materials are to be deposited. The area is displayed as a rectangle, from 1 to 7 fields in size.  
                   
  Water  
                   
  To flood a low-lying area with water, use the tab "Special", then click on "Water". The longer you left-click on the area, the higher the water level  rises. If the water level gets too high, you can lower it by pressing the left mouse button and the SHIFT key simultaneously.
Use this procedure with caution, otherwise too much land could be flooded and that will hinder your own progress.		  
  If you mistakenly flood too much land, use the "Undo Water" switch. This reverses the last action. But remember, you can only undo once per operation!

Important!!

The water model is extremely realistic, so much so that if you flood a strongly-contoured area (with hills, fissures) then lower the water level, that water will run into fissures and low-lying areas to form small lakes!

  
                   
  Trees  
                   
 

Different types of trees make the game area much more realistic. First click on the "Objectsl" tab, then from the object list choose "Passives" and then from the passive objects list, choose trees. To remove trees and to specify the area  to be to planted,  just repeat the process from the ,"Resources" section

                   
     
                   
  Wrecks and Ruins  
                   
 

The Editor makes it possible for you to simulate a battlefield by introducing unit wrecks and ruined buildings  into an area. Use the "Objects" tab, then from the object list choose "Passives" and then from the passive object list choose wrecks. Introduce ruined buildings in the same way.

 
                   
  Bridges  
                   
  A particular location will often be inaccessible. To reach a far shore, for example, you can build a bridge. Choose one of the available types (using the "Objects" tab then from the first list choose "Roads, bridges etc." and from the list below  choose "Bridges". From the list on the right you can choose one of the bridge types) and put it on the terrain. Using "+" "-" buttons you can increase and decrease the number of bridge elements  (every bridge consists of several simple elements).

The easiest way to build bridges:

- choose a bridge type,

- put the required number of bridge elements on the flat terrain. (You can make the bridges as long as you wish),

- choose "Flat" from the "Altitudes" tab,

- hold down the "SHIFT" key and left mouse button to lower the terrain under the bridge.

 
     
                   
  Tunnels  
                   
  The game is played on two levels. The subterranean level is in your tunnels. You can’t construct anything here, but a tunnel can get you close to an enemy base or even get you right into the enemy base itself. Use the "Tunnels" tab and choose tunnel type and width. The types are listed in 4 columns. Each single column represents switches 1 to 16.  
  Each type of tunnel (Type 1..4) have 4 variations in the following groups: 1 - 4, 5 - 8, 9 - 12 and 13 -16. Each switch symbolizes a particular wall and floor version. Clicking on several, for example, 1, 2, 3 and 4, will give you a random distribution. There are 3 adjacent switches for selecting single, double and treble tunnel widths.  
                   
  Starting Points  
                   
 

When all the above items etc., have been placed in the game area, it’s time to determine the starting points, i.e., the point from which each player starts his/her particular game. You must do this to be able to save the game later. The starting point for the first player (0) is automaticly placed when you create a map. For other players you must place starting points manually.  
                   
  Saving the Game Map  
                   
  So, you’ve done everything and are ready to start! Now save the Game Map by clicking on "Menu" and "Save". Now just type in the map name of your choice. This means you can now play Skirmish or Multiplayer. Good luck, and have lots of fun!!  
   
                   
  Trains  
                   
  To make your map more atractive you may include railway lines with moving or stationary trains.

 Important imformation about constructing railroads:

- place rails only on flat terrain

TIP: Construct your railroad before modeling a new map.

On the generated map you may flaten terrain by using the 'Const' option from the 'Altitudes' tab.

Trains in World War III may behave as follow:

N (Nothing) - train remains stationary

B (To the end of railroad and back) - train runs  between the begining and the and of  the railroad.

L (Around the World, looped) - train runs beyond the map, and  appears on the opposite side of the map.

Remember!

Railroads can only run North <->  South or East <-> West.

Constructing a railroad:

Choose 'Objects' tab, from the combo box choose 'Railroads and Trains' , then  select 
'Railway long' or 'Railway'. Place the railway lines on the map.

If you want your train to run to the end of map (L behavior), rails must be placed from 
left margin <-> right margin or down margin <-> up margin.

When the railroad is ready, a train may be put on it. Choose 'Objects' tab, from the combo box choose 'Railroads and Trains' , from the drop down list choose 'Trains', and then from  right list select e.g. 'US locomotive'. Now you must characterize its behaviour, click on the 'N', 'B' or 'L' button.

Now you can put the train on the rails.

Remember!

If you choose B behaviour, your train must  have two locomotives (look at the screen).
